First time here? What this page is (30 seconds)

This is a free, volunteer-run page from the Holly Avenue PTA that watches Arcadia USD and Sacramento so you don't have to. A robot checks the district's news, board meetings, and California education law every night; a PTA officer turns anything that matters into the plain-English cards below. No app, no account, no notifications — just bookmark it.

How to read it: Coming up = decisions with dates on them. In effect now = rules already live at school. Parents can act = the few things worth your time. Every card says what changes for your kid, who actually decides, and links its source — if a claim has no receipt, it doesn't go up here. Meetings have 📅 buttons that drop straight into your phone's calendar.

Heard a rumor? Search it — the link at the bottom of this page digs through everything ever tracked, back to the beginning. Still nothing? Ask at a PTA meeting; if it's real, it becomes a card.

The "VP Desk" tab is the officer's workbench — the raw feed behind these cards. It's open to read; only PTA officers can change what's published.

How this desk works (read this once — it's also the handoff manual)

Expected next is the job's known obligations with due dates. A red line means something should exist by now and nobody has verified it — click its check link, confirm reality, and the officer marks it done in the data file. A blank where a report should be is a finding, not a nothing.

The feed below is everything the nightly robot caught: district first (AUSD tag), then state. ● = priority. Bill numbers link to the actual statute on the Legislature's site — news is somebody's interpretation; the law is the law. Board meetings carry 📅 add-to-calendar links.

Search digs through every item ever tracked, not just what's on screen, grouped by month. Generate PTA report builds the paste-ready monthly update: district section first, receipts included.

Health: the "Sources reporting" line names any feed that's gone quiet; a red STALE warning means the nightly job itself stopped. Both are loud on purpose — this tool's only real failure mode is silent staleness.

Publishing happens in the repo, not on this page: the officer edits the data files (the repo README's operating manual walks through every task). The page itself is read-only for everyone.
