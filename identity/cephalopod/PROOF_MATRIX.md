# Proof matrix v2

The self-contained live page renders sixteen explicit run-length-encoded bitmap maps embedded in `index.html`. Each candidate has independent 32×32, 24×24, 16×16, and 12×12 rows. The smaller sizes are topology-resolved maps rather than raster reductions.

## Sheet 1: source residue translations

- Pose fan transfers independent limb articulation and pose-space diversity.
- Silhouette economy transfers figure-ground and negative-space separation.
- Module assembly transfers grouped parts, palette roles, and readable cell construction.
- Layer collision transfers intact but incompatible SimCity screen modules.

## Sheet 2: topology divergence

- Low anchor concentrates weight and short load-bearing arms.
- Side crawl turns the organism into a forward-moving profile.
- Buried archipelago hides continuity under substrate and re-emerges through openings.
- Distributed hub minimizes the central mass and maximizes differentiated arm reach.

## Sheet 3: substrate failure mechanisms

- Register shift displaces complete terrain bands.
- Tile duplicate repeats one structural cell family into a route.
- Aperture pair removes bounded cells and turns absence into passage.
- Palette overlay marks a second ordered terrain state without destroying geometry.

## Sheet 4: integrated Venn extremes

- Prior control retains the strongest residue from the earlier direction.
- Anchor dominant tests physical continuity and leverage.
- Local intelligence gives each arm a separate opportunity and contact.
- Emergent mastery hides the body in an archipelago while connected arms reappear through multiple routes.

The rejection test is explicit: a design fails when it reads as one generic upright octopus facing a vertical obstacle, or when palette changes are the only meaningful difference.
